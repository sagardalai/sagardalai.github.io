Monitoring our waters shouldn‚Äôt depend on slow, expensive field campaigns.

Environmental monitoring is often limited by boat time, manual sampling and scattered data. Harmful algal blooms can appear and disappear quickly, coastal ecosystems are dynamic, and traditional surveys are not always frequent or detailed enough to capture what is happening.

At the same time, we now have powerful UAV platforms, compact multispectral and hyperspectral sensors, and enough compute power at the edge to process data in real time. The challenge is turning all of this into reliable, automated systems that scientists, engineers and policymakers can trust.

My research goal: build end-to-end frameworks that use UAVs, AI and advanced imaging to map and monitor water quality and coastal environments, in a way that is repeatable, scalable and explainable.

I work across the full pipeline ‚Äî from raw data to ecological insights.

1
Smart data collection with UAVs & robotics
I design and use UAV workflows (e.g. DJI platforms, custom payloads) for coastal and agricultural monitoring, combining RGB, multispectral and hyperspectral sensors. This includes flight planning, radiometric calibration and integrating onboard compute for autonomous missions.
2
Advanced image & signal processing
My work uses tools like Agisoft Metashape, ENVI and custom Python pipelines to turn raw imagery into orthomosaics, point clouds and spectral products. I explore techniques such as index-based analysis, clustering and 3D reconstruction to derive biophysical parameters and water quality indicators.
3
Deep learning & novel architectures
I experiment with modern ML architectures for hyperspectral data, such as lightweight Vision Transformers and capsule networks (e.g. TinyCapsViT for HSI classification), as well as active learning and reinforcement learning strategies to reduce annotation cost.
4
Robust, controllable UAV behaviour
Beyond perception, I also work on UAV control and autonomy ‚Äî for example, discrete-time PID control with online gain adaptation for stable path following, and planning frameworks for multi-drone search and rescue in forests.
